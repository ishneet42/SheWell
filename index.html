PCOD and BMI

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOD) is a condition that can be influenced by various factors, including lifestyle and weight. Maintaining a healthy Body Mass Index (BMI) is crucial for managing PCOD effectively.

BMI is a numerical value of a person's weight in relation to their height. For individuals with PCOD, maintaining a BMI within the normal range is beneficial for hormonal balance and overall well-being.

The World Health Organization (WHO) defines BMI categories as follows:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 to 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 to 29.9
  • Obese: BMI 30 or greater

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional to determine the most suitable BMI range for your specific situation. Achieving and maintaining a healthy weight through a balanced diet, regular exercise, and lifestyle modifications can positively impact PCOD symptoms and overall health.
